2.2. Bernoulli Distribution

  1. Let YYY be a dichotomous random variable (takes one of two mutually exclusive values)

  2. Successes (= 1) occur with probability ppp and failures (= 0) occur with probability 1p1-p1p, for constant p[0,1]p \in [0,1]p[0,1]

  3. Notation: YBern(p)Y \sim Bern(p)YBern(p)

  4. Let Y be a dichotomous random variable representing a coin flip

    • Y=1Y = 1Y=1: heads, success
    • Y=0Y = 0Y=0: tails, fail
    • If the coin has a 60% chance to get the head/success
    • E(Y)=1p+0(1p)=pE(Y) = 1\cdot p + 0\cdot(1-p) = pE(Y)=1p+0(1p)=p
    • E(Y2)=12(p)+02(1p)=pE(Y^2) = 1^2\cdot(p) + 0^2\cdot(1-p) = pE(Y2)=12(p)+02(1p)=p
    • var(Y)=Y2=E(Y2)E(Y)2=pp2=p(1p)var(Y) = \sigma^2_{Y} = E(Y^2) - E(Y)^2 = p - p^2 = p(1-p)var(Y)=Y2=E(Y2)E(Y)2=pp2=p(1p)

2.3. Binomial Distribution

  1. Definition: If we have a sequence of nnn Bernoulli variables, each with a probability of success ppp, then the total number of successes is a binomial random variable.
    • Assumptions: fixed number of trials, independent, constant p
  2. Notation: XBin(n,p)X \sim Bin(n,p)XBin(n,p)
  3. Note for Combination and Permutation
    1. Combination: C(n,k)C(n,k)C(n,k) or (kn)\mathrm(^n_k)(kn)
    2. Permutation: P(n,k)P(n,k)P(n,k)
  4. Probability Mass Function:
    1. Pr(X=x)=(xn)px(1p)nxPr(X = x) = \mathrm(^n_x) \cdot p^{x} \cdot (1-p)^{n-x}Pr(X=x)=(xn)px(1p)nx
    2. Pr(X=x)=C(n,k)px(1p)nxPr(X = x) = C(n,k) \cdot p^{x} \cdot (1-p)^{n-x}Pr(X=x)=C(n,k)px(1p)nx
  5. Then if you flip coin for 100 times, n=100n = 100n=100, the probability to get head for k times is Pr(X=x)=C(100,k)pk(1p)100kPr(X = x) = C(100, k) \cdot p^{k} (1-p)^{100-k}Pr(X=x)=C(100,k)pk(1p)100k
  6. How do you calculate it in R?

    2. Calculate Pr(Xx)Pr(X \le x)Pr(Xx) using pbinom(x, n, p)
    3. Calculate Pr(Xx)Pr(X \ge x)Pr(Xx) using 1 - pbinom(x - 1, n, p)
  7. Summary measures
    1. Expection E(X)=npE(X) = npE(X)=np
    2. Variance var(X)=X2=np(1p)var(X) = \sigma^2_X = np(1-p)var(X)=X2=np(1p)

  8. How do you get those above:
    1. Consider Binomial Distribution as the sum of nnn times of Bernoulli Experiments

      1. E(X)=pE(X) = pE(X)=p
